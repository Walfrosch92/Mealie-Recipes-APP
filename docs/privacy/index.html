We don't collect any personal data

Mealie Recipes does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to the developer or to third parties. There are no user accounts with the developer, no analytics tool, no crash reporting, and no advertising networks. All your recipes, meal plans, and shopping lists live exclusively on your own Mealie server.

2

Connection to your own server

Mealie Recipes connects only to the Mealie server address that you enter yourself. All content – recipes, meal plans, shopping lists, and images – is exchanged directly between your device and your own server.

There is no developer-operated server through which this traffic passes. What data your Mealie server stores and how it processes it is entirely up to you as the operator of that server.

3

Local storage on your device

So you don't have to sign in again on every launch, the app stores the following information locally on your device:

  • Server address and access token of your Mealie instance – encrypted in the secure system storage (iOS Keychain / Android Keystore).
  • App settings (e.g. language, appearance) in local preferences.
  • Cached recipe data and images, so the app loads faster and works partly offline.

This data does not leave your device (other than the direct communication with your own server) and can be removed completely by uninstalling the app.

4

No tracking, no ads, no analytics

The app contains no analytics, tracking, or advertising components. No usage statistics are collected, no advertising IDs are read, and no behavior is logged.

5

No data sharing

Because no personal data is collected, no data is sold, rented, or shared with third parties.

6

Permissions & technologies used

The app only requests the permissions it needs for its features, each for a specific purpose:

  • Network / Internet: to connect to your Mealie server.
  • Local network (Bonjour / mDNS): to discover other devices on your Wi-Fi for “Cook Friends” and “Recipe Send” (peer-to-peer) – without any internet or cloud connection.
  • Camera & Photos / Media: only when you take or pick a photo for recipe import.
  • Notifications, exact alarms & full-screen intent: for cooking timers and their alarm.
  • Foreground service (Android): so timers keep running reliably.
  • Biometrics (Face ID / Touch ID / fingerprint): optional app lock. The check is performed solely by the operating system; no biometric data is transmitted to the app or the developer.
  • Keep screen awake: so the screen doesn't turn off while in cooking mode.
  • Microphone (iOS): The app does not record any audio. This entry is only technically required because the audio framework used for the timer alarms references the microphone API – it is never requested.
Technology used

Mealie Recipes is built with Flutter and uses well-established open-source components that work exclusively locally or for the connection to your own server – including:

HTTP (dio) Secure Storage Local Notifications Biometrics mDNS (Bonsoir) Image Cache

There are no analytics, advertising, or crash-reporting SDKs included.

Optional third-party features
  • Apple Reminders (iOS): If you import shopping items from Apple Reminders, the app accesses your lists locally through Apple. None of this data is sent to the developer.
  • Google Tasks / Google Sign-In: If you choose to sync your shopping list with Google Tasks, you sign in with your Google account and the app communicates with the Google Tasks API. In that case Google's Privacy Policy additionally applies. This feature is optional and active only when you explicitly use it.
